# Seven Sisters Academy — Design Brainstorm

## Context
International baking and culinary food school. The brand should evoke warmth, craftsmanship, artisanal excellence, and global sophistication.

## Idea 1: "Old World Patisserie" — French Artisan Elegance

**Design Movement**: Art Nouveau meets Parisian Patisserie Aesthetic

**Core Principles**:
1. Warmth through aged parchment tones and deep burgundy accents
2. Handcrafted feel via serif typography and organic, flowing lines
3. Culinary heritage storytelling — every section feels like a page from a recipe journal
4. Generous negative space that lets ingredients and dishes breathe

**Color Philosophy**:
- Background: warm cream/parchment `#FAF6EE`
- Primary: deep burgundy/wine `#6B1E2E`
- Accent: burnished gold `#C49A3C`
- Text: dark espresso `#2C1A0E`
- Emotional intent: nostalgia, warmth, luxury, trust

**Layout Paradigm**:
- Asymmetric editorial layout — text columns offset against full-bleed imagery
- Staggered card grids for courses (not uniform rows)
- Diagonal section dividers using SVG wave/ribbon shapes

**Signature Elements**:
1. Thin gold rule lines as decorative separators
2. Drop-cap initials on key paragraphs
3. Circular medallion-style badges for certifications/awards

**Interaction Philosophy**:
- Hover reveals ingredient illustrations floating up from cards
- Smooth parallax scrolling on hero food photography
- Accordion-style course details that unfurl like a menu

**Animation**:
- Fade-in with slight upward drift (20px, 400ms ease-out)
- Gold rule lines draw in from left on scroll
- Card hover: subtle lift with warm shadow bloom

**Typography System**:
- Display: Playfair Display (serif, bold italic for headings)
- Body: Lora (serif, comfortable reading weight)
- Accent labels: Cormorant Garamond (elegant small caps)
</text>

## Idea 2: "Modern Culinary Studio" — Scandinavian Minimalism + Craft

**Design Movement**: Nordic Minimalism meets Contemporary Culinary Arts

**Core Principles**:
1. Radical clarity — every element earns its place
2. Texture through photography, not decoration
3. Bold typographic hierarchy as the primary visual language
4. Warm off-white surfaces with charcoal and terracotta accents

**Color Philosophy**:
- Background: warm off-white `#F7F4F0`
- Primary: deep charcoal `#1C1C1C`
- Accent: terracotta/clay `#C4603A`
- Secondary: sage green `#7A8C6E`
- Emotional intent: modern, clean, trustworthy, artisanal

**Layout Paradigm**:
- Horizontal split layouts (text left, image right) alternating per section
- Full-width text banners with oversized typography
- Masonry-style gallery for student work

**Signature Elements**:
1. Thick left-border accent lines in terracotta
2. Oversized section numbers (01, 02, 03) as visual anchors
3. Circular crop photography frames

**Interaction Philosophy**:
- Cursor-following ingredient particle effects on hero
- Image zoom on hover with smooth scale transition
- Sticky navigation that transitions from transparent to solid

**Animation**:
- Staggered text reveal per word (80ms delay each)
- Section entrance: clip-path wipe from left
- Button hover: background fill sweep from left

**Typography System**:
- Display: DM Serif Display (elegant, high contrast)
- Body: DM Sans (clean, modern, readable)
- Labels: Space Mono (monospaced for technical/course details)
</text>

## Idea 3: "International Culinary Heritage" — Global Warmth + Academic Prestige

**Design Movement**: Editorial Magazine meets Culinary Institute Prestige

**Core Principles**:
1. International feel through rich, diverse food photography
2. Academic credibility via structured layout and formal typography
3. Warmth through amber, saffron, and deep forest tones
4. Layered depth — overlapping elements, textured backgrounds

**Color Philosophy**:
- Background: deep forest green `#1A2E1E` (dark theme)
- Surface: warm dark card `#243028`
- Primary accent: saffron/amber `#E8A020`
- Text: warm ivory `#F5F0E8`
- Highlight: copper rose `#C47860`
- Emotional intent: prestige, international, warmth, excellence

**Layout Paradigm**:
- Magazine-style multi-column grid with varying column spans
- Hero section with overlapping text on image (editorial style)
- Horizontal scrolling course showcase strip

**Signature Elements**:
1. Saffron underline strokes on key headings
2. Textured dark green background with subtle grain overlay
3. Gold-bordered pull-quote callouts

**Interaction Philosophy**:
- Horizontal scroll for featured courses (like a magazine spread)
- Parallax depth layers in hero section
- Hover on course cards reveals program details with slide-up panel

**Animation**:
- Hero text: letter-by-letter reveal with 30ms stagger
- Section backgrounds: subtle Ken Burns effect on images
- Card hover: copper border glow + content slide up

**Typography System**:
- Display: Cormorant Garamond (high-contrast, editorial serif)
- Body: Source Sans 3 (clean, international readability)
- Accent: Cinzel (classical, academic for headings/labels)
</text>

## Selected Approach: **Idea 3 — "International Culinary Heritage"**

Dark forest green with saffron and copper accents, editorial magazine layout, Cormorant Garamond + Source Sans 3 typography. This best captures the international prestige and warmth of a world-class culinary academy.
